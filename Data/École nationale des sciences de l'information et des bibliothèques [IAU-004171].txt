History
Heir to the École nationale supérieure des bibliothécaires, created in 1963, Enssib is a public institution created by Decree No. 92–25 of 9 January 1992. It operates under the auspices of the French Ministry of Higher Education and Research and is administered by a Board of Directors and a Scientific Council.
Enssib trains managers in information, documentation and library sciences for the public and private sectors, through a combination of initial and continuing education. Enssib also puts great store on research as well as on promoting science and culture, particularly through its publications, its events program and the distribution of its open access resources.
It provides :
- initial statutory training for civil servants destined for scientific and supervisory functions in libraries (State curators, state librarians, and the city of Paris' Librarians).
- a lifelong training program in the field of information science and libraries, on site and remotely.
- three master's courses under the heading "Information and Library Sciences" (digital archives; scientific and technical information; library and documentation policy),
- a master's course under the heading "History, civilizations, heritage" (cultures of writing and image)
- a “Practices and methods in Digital Humanities” course under the heading “Digital Humanities”.
It also provides two establishment diplomas, all or part of which are carried out remotely:
- the diploma of operational framework of libraries and documentation and
- the university degree in information and library sciences offered jointly with the Senghor University of Alexandria.
All of these training courses fall within a highly coordinated field of training entitled Data, documents, digital and temporalities.
