History
Founded 1855 as Agricultural College of the State of Michigan. Successively renamed State Agricultural College 1861, Michigan Agricultural College 1909, Michigan State College of Agriculture and Applied Science 1925, Michigan State University of Agriculture and Applied Science 1955. Acquired present title 1964. Fully integrated the MSU College of Law as a constituent college 2020
Academic Year
August to May. Also Summer Session (May-August)
Admission Requirements
Graduation from high school or equivalent, and College Board SAT or ACT examination. TOEFL test for foreign students
