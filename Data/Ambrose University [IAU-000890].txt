History
Founded in 1921 as the Calgary Bible Institute. Became an accredited university college in 1995 and changed its name in 1999 to Nazarene University College (NUC). In 2003, Canadian Bible College (CBC) and Canadian Theological Seminary (CTS) moved to join NUC on the same campus in 2003, received accreditation in 2004, and changed their name to Alliance University College (AUC). AUC and NUC maintained a close relationship, began offering joint programs in 2006, and were often referred to as Alliance University College-Nazarene University College (AUC-NUC), although the two institutions awarded degrees independently until 2007.In 2007, AUC and NUC formed a single entity called Ambrose University. In 2014, province of Alberta granted 'university' nomenclature to Ambrose - Ambrose University College is renamed Ambrose University
