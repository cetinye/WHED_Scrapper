History
Founded by Bull of Pope Martin V on the initiative of Duke John of Brabant in 1425. Collegium Trilingue established by Erasmus in 1517. Suppressed under French occupation and closed during the reigns of Napoleon and William I of Holland. Re-established as Catholic University by Belgian episcopate in 1834. Reorganized with two divisions in 1969, the Katholieke Universiteit te Leuven and the Université catholique de Louvain which became separate legal entities in 1970. The titular head of the University is the Cardinal Archbishop of Malines. An institution independent of direct State control, receiving full state subvention
