History
Created in 1954 as a Public Health College and Training Centre (PHC & TC) in a joint effort between the Imperial Ethiopian government, WHO, United States Operation Mission to Ethiopia, and UNICEF, it quickly became established as a pioneer centre for public health practice serving as a key source of Ethiopia’s health professionals. It remains Ethiopia’s oldest health training institution.
Run by the Ministry of Health until 1960, the Training Centre joined Haile Selassie I University, now known as Addis Ababa University, in 1961 by an act of internal decree known as the “Charter of Haile Selassie I University”. In 1978, by bilateral agreement between Karl Marx University in Germany and Addis Ababa University, a medical faculty was established within the Training Centre- a major milestone in the establishment of medical education in the country.
In 1980/81 the Training Centre was renamed Gondar College of Medical Sciences (GCMS). GCMS gained autonomy from Addis Ababa University in 1992. In 2003 the College became Gondar University College and in 2004 the institution acquired its current title.
