History
Founded in 1946 as the Institute of Health for Higher Education, Shiraz University of Medical Sciences (SUMS) acquired its current title and status in 1986. With over 900 faculty members, 18 schools, 60 research centers and 14 university hospitals, SUMS is the leading institution for medical education in southern Iran. As well as being a center of learning in medical fields, it supervises and manages all public hospitals, clinics, and healthcare centers in Fars Province in southern Iran. It also oversees the private health sector across the province. The University is the main hub of Liver Transplantation, Clinical Immunology, Clinical Microbiology, and Advanced Electronic Learning.
