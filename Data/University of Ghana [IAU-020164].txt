History
Founded in 1948 as University College of the Gold Coast affiliated to the University of London, became University of Ghana after independence in 1957, and acquired present status and title in 1961
Academic Year
August to July (August-December; January-July)
Admission Requirements
General Certificate of Education (GCE) with 5 credits including English, Mathematics, Arts and Science or West Africa School Certificate (WASC) Ordinary ('O') level and three passes at Advanced ('A') level, with a minimum grade D for one. Senior Secondary School Certificate with passes in core English, Mathematics and any 3 elective subjects, with aggregate score of 24 in the WAEC entrance examination
