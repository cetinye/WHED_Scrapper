History
Founded 1899 as Western Illinois State Normal School, became Teachers' College 1921. Became Western Illinois State College 1947, and acquired present title 1957. A State institution.
Academic Year
August to May (August-December; January-May). Also Summer Sessions (June-July)
Admission Requirements
Graduation from high school or equivalent, ACT Score of 22 (SAT 910) or ACT of at least 18 (SAT 120), if they rank in upper 50% of their high school class. TOEFL test for foreign students
