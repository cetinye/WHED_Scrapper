History
IACA was founded by virtue of the Agreement for the Establishment of the International Anti-Corruption Academy as an International Organization and became an international organization on 8 March 2011. Under its headquarters agreement with the Republic of Austria of 8 March 2011, IACA is recognized by the Republic of Austria (the Federal Ministry for Science, Research and Economy, acting in its capacity as ENIC NARIC AUSTRIA) as an institution of post-secondary education.
Academic Year
October to September
Admission Requirements
Undergraduate degree, equivalent to a Bachelor’s degree. Minimum of three years of relevant work experience or a proven history of academic research in areas related to anti-corruption and/or compliance, broadly understood. Candidates who do not have working experience yet and are applying directly from the Bachelor’s degree should have their undergraduate degree qualification such as economics, law, political science, sociology, or any other filed related to the IACA academic programmes.
Proficiency in written and spoken English.
