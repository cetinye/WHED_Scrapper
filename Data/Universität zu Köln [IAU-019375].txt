History
Founded 1388 by the citizens of Cologne, closed 1798 and reopened 1919 including a Faculty of Management, Economics and Social Sciences as well as a Faculty of Medicine. One year later the Faculty of Law and the Faculty of Humanities were established. In 1955 the Faculty of Mathematics and Natural Sciences was created and in 1980 the Faculty of Human Sciences was added for the University to become one of the oldest and biggest comprehensive universities in Germany. Today UoC is one of the leading research univeristites in the country. It is under the jurisdiction of and financed by the State of North Rhine-Westphalia
