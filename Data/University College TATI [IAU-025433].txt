History
University College TATI (UC TATI), formerly known as Terengganu Advanced Technical Institute (TATI), was established on 11th June 1993 through a collaborative effort between the State Government of Terengganu and Ingenieurschule St. Gallen, Switzerland. From its inception, UC TATI has been dedicated to cultivating highly skilled technocrats in cutting-edge engineering disciplines and fostering top-tier talent for the workforce. A significant milestone in its journey occurred when UC TATI was officially recognized as a University College, cementing its commitment to excellence. On 17th September 2019, the Department of Higher Education (Malaysia) approved the institution's rebranding as University College TATI (UC TATI), marking a new chapter in its history. Since 1993, UC TATI has remained steadfast in its pursuit of global eminence, striving to produce well-rounded and industry-ready graduates. Presently, UC TATI offers a diverse array of 38 academic programs encompassing foundation, diploma, bachelor, and postgraduate levels to both local and international students. These programs span various fields including Manufacturing, CNC Machining, Electronics and Automation, Mechatronics, Robotics and Instrumentation, Chemical Engineering and Polymer, Computer Science, Computer Network & Security (CISCO), Graphic Design, Entrepreneurship, Accounting, and Business Management.
