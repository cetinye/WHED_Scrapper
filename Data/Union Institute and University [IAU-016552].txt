History
Founded 1964 as a consortium (The Union for Research and Experimentation in Higher Education) that included 10 liberal arts colleges, including Goddard College, Hofstra University, Bard College, Antioch College, and Sarah Lawrence College. Incorporated in the state of Ohio under the name of The Union for Experimenting Colleges and Universities (UECU) 1969. Was granted authorization as a degree-granting institution 1971 by the Ohio Board of Regents and was also granted candidate for accreditation status by the Commission on Institutions of Higher Education of the North Central Association (NCA) 1972. The consortium dissolved, with UECU remaining as a freestanding, not-for-profit institution of higher education 1982. Acquired current title 1989
