History
Founded in 1933, an internationally recognized institution training artists for a professional career in music, theatre, dance and film and offers related theoretical studies. Formerly Lietuvos Muzikos Akademija. In 1949 the Conservatoire of Kaunas and the Conservatoire of Vilnius (founded in 1945) were merged into the Lithuanian State Conservatoire. In 1992 the Conservatoire was renamed Lithuanian Academy of Music (LMA), and in 2004 Lithuanian Academy of Music and Theatre (LMTA)
